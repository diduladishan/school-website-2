Programme Introduction

IB Early Years

A nurturing start to inspire curiosity, creativity, and confidence in young learners. A foundation stage that blends play, inquiry, and exploration to build essential skills and a love for learning.

The Importance

of Play

Learning through exploration, imagination, and joyful discovery.

Play is at the heart of our teaching philosophy. Through purposeful play, children develop essential cognitive, social, and emotional skills. Whether building, creating, or role-playing, every activity is designed to enhance problem-solving abilities, spark imagination, and build positive learning experiences that last a lifetime.

A Learning Space

Designed for Growth

Our classrooms are carefully designed to stimulate young minds and provide a balance of structure and freedom. Learning centres encourage hands-on exploration, while collaborative spaces help children develop social skills and teamwork. Every element of the environment is crafted to promote inquiry, independence, and joyful discovery.

Inquiry-based learning in action
Multisensory, child-centered spaces
Inspiring environments that support creativity

Our Approach at

Dar Al Marefa

Empowering students through bilingual learning and inquiry-driven education.

At Dar Al Marefa, we combine the principles of the IBPYP with our unique approach to bilingual education. Children are introduced to both Arabic and English from the earliest years, promoting cultural understanding and communication skills. Our inquiry-driven curriculum ensures that every child is encouraged to explore, create, and thrive in a supportive, inclusive environment.

MEET THE TEAM

The

Early Learner

Nurturing curiosity, independence, and a love for discovery from the very beginning.

In the IBPYP Early Years, children are not just learners—they are inquirers, thinkers, and communicators. Our focus is on helping them develop the attitudes and attributes that will define their future learning journeys. With guidance from skilled educators, they build the confidence to express ideas, ask questions, and develop a deep love for learning that extends beyond the classroom.
