Programme Introduction

IB Middle Years

The MYP emphasizes a student-centered approach that blends traditional subjects with modern educational practices. Through structured inquiry and contextual learning, students develop the skills needed for success in school and beyond. Teachers facilitate exploration while students take increasing responsibility for their own learning.

Emphasis on conceptual understanding
Teaching and learning in real-world contexts
Development of approaches to learning (ATL) skills
Focus on service as action and student voice
Interdisciplinary learning opportunities

THE PYP

LEARNING MODEL

The PYP framework combines essential elements—knowledge, concepts, skills, attitudes, and action—to create a balanced learning experience. This model ensures that students not only gain academic understanding but also develop the values and attributes needed to become responsible global citizens.

THE MYP

Personal Project

The Personal Project is a cornerstone of the final year in the MYP, allowing students to explore an area of personal interest. This project promotes independent learning and reflection, requiring students to apply ATL skills and demonstrate initiative, organization, and critical thinking.
